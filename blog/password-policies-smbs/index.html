Stronger Password Policies Every SMB Should Enforce

March 17, 2025 by CES Team

passwords cybersecurity authentication smb

Introduction

Cybersecurity often begins with the simplest line of defense: the password. Yet time and again, businesses suffer breaches because employees reuse weak passwords like “Spring2025!” across multiple systems. For SMBs with limited resources, password policies are a cost-effective way to drastically reduce risk.

Why Passwords Matter

  • Over 60% of breaches involve stolen or weak credentials.
  • Cybercriminals use brute force tools that can guess billions of combinations in minutes.
  • A single compromised password can grant access to email, cloud storage, and even financial accounts.

Best Practices for SMB Password Policies

1. Require Length and Complexity

At least 12 characters, mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.

2. Ban Common and Reused Passwords

Use a password filter to block “123456,” “password,” and other known weak strings.

3. Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Even strong passwords can be stolen. MFA adds a second layer of defense.

4. Regular Expiration vs. Risk-Based Resets

Rather than forcing changes every 30 days, which encourages weak choices, reset after suspicious logins or confirmed breaches.

5. Promote Password Managers

Encourage employees to store credentials securely instead of reusing or writing them down.

Real-World Example

A local accounting firm suffered an email breach when a shared admin password was stolen. After adopting CES’s password policies and MFA, they eliminated similar incidents.
